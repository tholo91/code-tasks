# UX & Aesthetics

`code-tasks` is built on the principle that a tool's design should be as inspiring as the ideas it captures.

## Design Inspiration
- **Things 3**: For its minimal, high-fidelity UI and "one-task-at-a-time" focus.
- **Sorted 3**: For its binary priority system and efficient scheduling.

## Principles of Interaction
1. **Quick Capture**: No friction. One tap to create, one tap to sync.
2. **Intentional Motion**: Every animation should feel light and purposeful—guiding the user's attention.
3. **One-Handed Use**: All critical actions are easily reachable on mobile devices.

## Animation Guidelines
Animations should feel cohesive and reinforce the user's mental model:

- **New Task**: Slides in from the bottom and fades in, suggesting it's being added to the current view.
- **Archive/Delete**: Slides out to the right and collapses, visually indicating its removal.
- **Detail Panel**: Slides in from the right (on desktop) or bottom (on mobile), providing a deeper level of focus.
- **Done Task**: A smooth strikethrough animation accompanied by a subtle opacity fade.
- **Drag & Drop**: Cards lift with a subtle shadow, and list items shift fluidly to create space.

## Visual Styling
- **Clean Markdown**: The underlying file is always human-readable and well-formatted.
- **Minimalist Palette**: A focus on whitespace and typography, with subtle color accents for priority and tags.
- **Binary Priority Pills**: Simple, effective indicators for "Standard" and "Important" tasks.
